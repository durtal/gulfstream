Turf

This is the track profile for all 7.5 furlong turf races at Gulfstream Park, there are 101 races in the dataset, contested by 993 runners. The oldest race in the dataset was run on 2012-12-05 while the most recent was run on 2014-12-28.

Summary

A few quick and dirty summary plots highlighting the different race types and field sizes. MDN CLM races are the most common over the 7.5 furlong course, with 33 races. The average field size for 7.5 furlong races is 9.83.

Draw

The 7.5 furlong course is run round two turns, the first turn starts almost immediately after horse jump from the starting gates, and continues for roughly 2 furlongs, the second turn ends roughly 1f from the finish line. Counting winners is the typical way to assess any draw bias, but winners ignore a large number of runners, and if there was any such bias, it should be seen across all runners, so percentage of runners beaten is to be used (gates must have a minimum of 5 runs):

A fair draw would expect to see each draw beat 50% of their rivals.

The draw will likely have an impact on the distance horses travel, especially as the first turn is such a short distance from the start. Horses drawn wide (high) will likely be forced wide. Unsurprisingly, horses drawn in gate 1 travel the least distance, with an average trip of 4998.03 feet, while horses draw in gate 12 travel an average distance of 5038.19, a difference of 40.16 feet (gates must have a minimum of 5 runs used). Race Shape

Race shape on turf courses isn’t quite as predictable as on dirt courses, as the surface allows horses who started slowly to finish faster (you will see the difference in profiles for 8f and 8.5f races on dirt and turf). The plot below shows the sectional speeds of winners, to be clear these aren’t cumulative speeds, but the speed from one sectional to the next (taking into account the distance travelled - Trakus data - for each horse), so from the start to 2f, from 2f to 3f, etc. Winners are still typically running slower over the final 0.5f but the fall off isn’t as sharp as seen on dirt, and the middle part of the race from 2f to ~6.5f sees runners showing consistent speed.

The fastest opening quarter by a horse who went on to win was by SHE’S SPOOKY on 24/01/14 in race 6, recording a speed of 60.17 feet per second. There were horses who ran faster, PETEY CRAMER ran the opening quarter in a speed of 60.62 ft per second.

The plots below show the distribution of 5f Finishing Speeds for winners compared to “The Rest”, and the opening 2f sectionals for winners compared to “The Rest”. The distributions are similar, not surprising to see “The Rest” with a fatter distribution in both plots, perhaps most interesting is that the winners not only record higher Finishing Speeds than “The Rest”, but they are also faster over the opening 2f. Winners, on average, run the opening 2f in 48.12, compared to “The Rest”, who run the opening 2f in 48.43, a difference of 0.31 seconds. For reference, the horses leading their race after 2f run the opening 2f in an average time of 47.69.

Plotting the opening 4f sectional against the closing 3.5f Finishing Speeds the difference becomes a little more clear. Runners aren’t necessarily punished if they start slowly as in sprint, or dirt, races, the kinder surface means horses are capable of finishing much faster and making up ground on horses who started faster.

The winner with the highest 3.5f Finishing Speed was MEDAL COUNT when winning a all op clm (race 9 on 12/01/14), after an opening 4f of 51.3 MEDAL COUNT recorded a Finishing Speed of 1.052. The winner with the fastest opening quarter was DEFIANT when winning a clm (race 10 on 28/03/14), recording a time of 44.41 over the opening half mile and a 3.5f Finishing Speed of 0.95.
