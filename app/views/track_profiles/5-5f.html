Dirt

This is the track profile for all 5.5 furlong dirt races at Gulfstream Park, there are 209 races in the dataset, contested by 1772 runners. The oldest race in the dataset was run on 2012-12-05 while the most recent was run on 2015-05-03.

Summary

A few quick and dirty summary plots highlighting the different race types and field sizes. CLM races are the most common over the 5.5 furlong course, with 82 races. The average field size for 5.5 furlong races is 8.48.

Draw

The 5.5 furlong course is run round one turn, which starts roughly ~1.5 furlongs from the starting gates, and continues for roughly 2 furlongs, meaning the home straight is ~1.5f. Counting winners is the typical way to assess any draw bias, but winners ignore a large number of runners, and if there was any such bias, it should be seen across all runners, so percentage of runners beaten is to be used (gates must have a minimum of 5 runs). A fair draw would expect to see each draw beat 50% of their rivals, runners draw in gates 1, 2 beat more than 55% of their rivals.

The draw will likely have an impact on the distance horses travel, especially as the first and only turn is such a short distance from the start. Horses drawn wide (high) will likely be forced wide. Unsurprisingly, horses drawn in gate 1 travel the least distance, with an average trip of 3332.05 feet, while horses draw in gate 11 travel an average distance of 3355.15, a difference of 23.1 feet (gates must have a minimum of 5 runs used). Race Shape

Race shape is something that is far easier to highlight at sprint distances, they are the fastest run races and as such take on a similar shape. That is, by and large, all runners are slowing at the finish, almost irrespective of the pace they showed early in the race - of course the extent of the slowing will be down to pace. The plot below shows the sectional speeds of winners, to be clear these aren’t cumulative speeds, but the speed from one sectional to the next (taking into account the distance travelled - Trakus data - for each horse), so from the start to 2f, from 2f to 3f, etc. As shown, winners are slowing all the way to the finish, runners behind all show the same fatigue late only more sharply.

The fastest opening quarter by a horse who went on to win was by MUNNINGS SISTER on 06/02/14 in race 9, recording a speed of 61.84 feet per second. There were horses who ran faster, R FREE ROLL ran the opening quarter in a speed of 61.86 ft per second. Both horses were competing in the same STK race.

Pace

The plots below show the distribution of 4f Finishing Speeds for winners compared to “The Rest”, and the opening 2f sectionals for winners compared to “The Rest”. The distributions are similar, not surprising to see “The Rest” with a fatter distribution in both plots, perhaps most interesting is that the winners not only record higher Finishing Speeds than “The Rest”, but they are also faster over the opening 2f. Winners, on average, run the opening 2f in 22.78, compared to “The Rest”, who run the opening 2f in 23.22, a difference of 0.44 seconds. For reference, the horses leading their race after 2f run the opening 2f in an average time of 22.58.

Plotting the opening 2f sectional against the closing 3.5f Finishing Speeds the difference becomes a little more clear. The cloud of points is crowded but winners are, for the majority, in the upper left part of the cloud.

The winner with the highest 3.5f Finishing Speed was BLACK HEIR when winning a mdn clm (race 2 on 26/01/13), after an opening quarter of 23.91 BLACK HEIR recorded a Finishing Speed of 1.007. The winner with the fastest opening quarter was MUNNINGS SISTER when winning a stk (race 9 on 06/02/14), recording a time of 21.49 over the opening quarter and a 3.5f Finishing Speed of 0.965.
