Dirt

This is the track profile for all 7 furlong dirt races at Gulfstream Park, there are 205 races in the dataset, contested by 1613 runners. The oldest race in the dataset was run on 2012-12-01 while the most recent was run on 2014-10-04.

Summary

A few quick and dirty summary plots highlighting the different race types and field sizes. CLM races are the most common over the 7 furlong course, with 77 races. The average field size for 7 furlong races is 7.87.

Draw

The 7 furlong course is run round one turn, which starts roughly 3 furlongs from the starting gates, and continues for roughly 2 furlongs, meaning the home straight is ~2f. Counting winners is the typical way to assess any draw bias, but winners ignore a large number of runners, and if there was any such bias, it should be seen across all runners, so percentage of runners beaten is to be used (gates must have a minimum of 5 runs):

A fair draw would expect to see each draw beat 50% of their rivals.

The draw will likely have an impact on the distance horses travel, especially as the first and only turn is such a short distance from the start. Horses drawn wide (high) will likely be forced wide. Unsurprisingly, horses drawn in gate 1 travel the least distance, with an average trip of 4664.85 feet, while horses draw in gate 12 travel an average distance of 4692.71, a difference of 27.86 feet (gates must have a minimum of 5 runs used). Race Shape

Race shape is often quite stable on dirt courses, as the surface sees horses slowing at the finish. That is, by and large, all runners are slowing at the finish, almost irrespective of the pace they showed early in the race - of course the extent of the slowing will be down to pace. The plot below shows the sectional speeds of winners, to be clear these aren’t cumulative speeds, but the speed from one sectional to the next (taking into account the distance travelled - Trakus data - for each horse), so from the start to 2f, from 2f to 3f, etc. As shown, winners are slowing all the way to the finish, runners behind all show the same fatigue late only more sharply.

The fastest opening quarter by a horse who went on to win was by ANILLO on 04/07/13 in race 6, recording a speed of 59.85 feet per second. There were horses who ran faster, SINGANOTHERSONG ran the opening quarter in a speed of 60.22 ft per second.

Pace

Finishing Speeds also reflect the slowing to the finish shown in the Race Shape plot above, this will in part be due to the dirt surface (horses slow at the finish over longer trips also). The par finishing speeds for 7f winners are:

The plots below show the distribution of 5f Finishing Speeds for winners compared to “The Rest”, and the opening 2f sectionals for winners compared to “The Rest”. The distributions are similar, not surprising to see “The Rest” with a fatter distribution in both plots, perhaps most interesting is that the winners not only record higher Finishing Speeds than “The Rest”, but they are also faster over the opening 2f. Winners, on average, run the opening 2f in 23.17, compared to “The Rest”, who run the opening 2f in 23.41, a difference of 0.24 seconds. For reference, the horses leading their race after 2f run the opening 2f in an average time of 22.94.

Plotting the opening 2f sectional against the closing 5f Finishing Speeds the difference becomes a little more clear. The cloud of points is crowded but winners are, for the majority, in the upper left part of the cloud.

The winner with the highest 4.5f Finishing Speed was JANE OF ALL TRADES when winning a all op clm (race 10 on 06/03/14), after an opening quarter of 24.12 JANE OF ALL TRADES recorded a Finishing Speed of 1.005. The winner with the fastest opening quarter was ANILLO when winning a all op clm (race 6 on 04/07/13), recording a time of 22.19 over the opening quarter and a 4f Finishing Speed of 0.973.
