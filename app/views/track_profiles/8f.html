Dirt

This is the track profile for all 8 furlong dirt races at Gulfstream Park, there are 417 races in the dataset, contested by 3348 runners. The oldest race in the dataset was run on 2012-12-02 while the most recent was run on 2015-01-04.

Summary

A few quick and dirty summary plots highlighting the different race types and field sizes. CLM races are the most common over the 8 furlong course, with 199 races. The average field size for 8 furlong races is 8.03.

Draw

The 8 furlong course is run round two turns, the first turn starts soon after horses jump from the starting gates, and continues for roughly 2 furlongs, the second turn ends roughly 2f from the finish line. Counting winners is the typical way to assess any draw bias, but winners ignore a large number of runners, and if there was any such bias, it should be seen across all runners, so percentage of runners beaten is to be used (gates must have a minimum of 5 runs):

A fair draw would expect to see each draw beat 50% of their rivals, runners draw in gate 1 beat more than 55% of their rivals.

The draw will likely have an impact on the distance horses travel, especially as the first turn is such a short distance from the start. Horses drawn wide (high) will likely be forced wide. Unsurprisingly, horses drawn in gate 1 travel the least distance, with an average trip of 5330.72 feet, while horses draw in gate 12 travel an average distance of 5348.52, a difference of 17.8 feet (gates must have a minimum of 5 runs used). The plot below shows boxplots of distance raced by horses in each starting gate.

Race Shape

Race shape on dirt is quite consistent even across a variety of distances, as the surface rarely allows horses who started slowly to finish faster (you will see the difference in affect looking at 8f turf race shape). The plot below shows the sectional speeds of winners, to be clear these arent cumulative speeds, but the speed from one sectional to the next (taking into account the distance travelled - Trakus data - for each horse), so from the start to 2f, from 2f to 3f, etc. Winners are slowing at the finish, but as significantly as seen in dirt sprints, but still slowing.

The fastest opening half mile by a horse who went on to win was by BLACK DIAMOND CAT on 14/09/13 in race 7, recording a speed of 60.82 feet per second. There were horses who ran faster, TOUCHOFSTARQUALITY ran the opening 4f in a speed of 61.41 ft per second.

The plots below show the distribution of 4f Finishing Speeds for winners compared to The Rest, and the opening 4f sectionals for winners compared to The Rest. The distributions are similar, especially the opening 4f where winners dont really differentiate themselves to The Rest as we saw in dirt sprints. Winners, on average, run the opening 4f in 47.12, compared to The Rest, who run the opening 2f in 47.49, a difference of 0.37 seconds. For reference, the horses leading their race after 2f run the opening 2f in an average time of 46.84. It is the ability to carry that early speed to the finish that sees winners separate themselves from The Rest.

Plotting the opening 4f sectional against the closing 4f Finishing Speeds the difference becomes a little more clear. Unlike dirt sprints where Winners were seen to be running faster early, over 8 furlong races Winners can make up ground they may have lost early on tiring rivals more easily.

The winner with the highest 4f Finishing Speed was DREAMLICIOUS when winning a all op clm (race 7 on 11/05/14), after an opening 4f of 52.26 DREAMLICIOUS recorded a Finishing Speed of 1.029. The winner with the fastest opening half mile was ITSAKNOCKOUT when winning a all op clm (race 4 on 04/01/15), recording a time of 44.84 over the opening half mile and a 4f Finishing Speed of 0.946.

Turf

This is the track profile for all 8 furlong turf races at Gulfstream Park, there are 304 races in the dataset, contested by 3034 runners. The oldest race in the dataset was run on 2012-12-01 while the most recent was run on 2015-01-04.

Summary

A few quick and dirty summary plots highlighting the different race types and field sizes. MDN CLM races are the most common over the 8 furlong course, with 87 races. The average field size for 8 furlong races is 9.98.

Draw

The 8 furlong course is run round two turns, the first turn starts soon after horses jump from the starting gates, and continues for roughly 2 furlongs, the second turn ends roughly 2f from the finish line. Counting winners is the typical way to assess any draw bias, but winners ignore a large number of runners, and if there was any such bias, it should be seen across all runners, so percentage of runners beaten is to be used (gates must have a minimum of 5 runs):

A fair draw would expect to see each draw beat 50% of their rivals, runners draw in gate 14 beat more than 55% of their rivals.

The draw will likely have an impact on the distance horses travel, especially as the first turn is such a short distance from the start. Horses drawn wide (high) will likely be forced wide. Unsurprisingly, horses drawn in gate 1 travel the least distance, with an average trip of 5337.41 feet, while horses draw in gate 14 travel an average distance of 5371.33, a difference of 33.92 feet (gates must have a minimum of 5 runs used). The plot below shows boxplots of distance raced by horses in each starting gate.

Race Shape

Race shape on turf courses isnt quite as predictable as on dirt courses, as the surface allows horses who started slowly to finish faster (you will see the difference in profiles for 8f and 8.5f races on dirt and turf). The plot below shows the sectional speeds of winners, to be clear these arent cumulative speeds, but the speed from one sectional to the next (taking into account the distance travelled - Trakus data - for each horse), so from the start to 2f, from 2f to 3f, etc. Winners are still typically running slower over the final 0.5f but the fall off isnt as sharp as seen on dirt, and the middle part of the race from 2f to ~6.5f sees runners showing consistent speed.

The fastest opening half mile by a horse who went on to win was by ZA APPROVAL on 30/03/13 in race 11, recording a speed of 59.94 feet per second. This opening half mile was the fastest speed by any horse, in victory or defeat.

Finishing Speeds also reflect the consistent speed throughout the race, only falling off in the last 0.5f.

The plots below show the distribution of 4f Finishing Speeds for winners compared to The Rest, and the opening 4f sectionals for winners compared to The Rest. The distributions for the opening 4f are very similar, Winners, on average, run the opening 4f in 48.38, compared to The Rest, who run the opening 4f in 48.62, a difference of 0.24 seconds. For reference, the horses leading their race after 4f run the opening 4f in an average time of 47.91. The 4f Finishing Speeds are quite different, with The Rest having a much fatter distribution. Winners average 4f Finishing Speed is above 1.000, meaning their final 4f is faster than their opening 4f, ie. they can just as easily win their races late.

Plotting the opening 4f sectional against the closing 4f Finishing Speeds the difference becomes a little more clear. Runners arent necessarily punished if they start slowly as in sprint, or dirt, races, the kinder surface means horses are capable of finishing much faster and making up ground on horses who started faster.

The winner with the highest 4f Finishing Speed was PARRANDA when winning a stk (race 8 on 28/09/13), after an opening 4f of 52.47 PARRANDA recorded a Finishing Speed of 1.055. The winner with the fastest opening half mile was MELLOW FELLOW when winning a all op clm (race 4 on 10/08/14), recording a time of 45.79 over the opening half mile and a 4f Finishing Speed of 0.976.
