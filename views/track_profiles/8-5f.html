Dirt

This is the track profile for all 8.5 furlong dirt races at Gulfstream Park, there are 201 races in the dataset, contested by 1598 runners. The oldest race in the dataset was run on 2012-12-01 while the most recent was run on 2015-05-06.

Summary

A few quick and dirty summary plots highlighting the different race types and field sizes. CLM races are the most common over the 8.5 furlong course, with 62 races. The average field size for 8.5 furlong races is 7.95.

Draw

The 8.5 furlong course is run round two turns, the first turn starts soon after horses jump from the starting gates, and continues for roughly 2 furlongs, the second turn ends roughly 2f from the finish line. Counting winners is the typical way to assess any draw bias, but winners ignore a large number of runners, and if there was any such bias, it should be seen across all runners, so percentage of runners beaten is to be used (gates must have a minimum of 5 runs). A fair draw would expect to see each draw beat 50% of their rivals, runners draw in gate 1 beat more than 55% of their rivals.

The draw will likely have an impact on the distance horses travel, especially as the first turn is such a short distance from the start. Horses drawn wide (high) will likely be forced wide. Unsurprisingly, horses drawn in gate 1 travel the least distance, with an average trip of 5679.31 feet, while horses draw in gate 10 travel an average distance of 5719.15, a difference of 39.84 feet (gates must have a minimum of 5 runs used). Race Shape

Race shape on dirt is quite consistent even across a variety of distances, as the surface rarely allows horses who started slowly to finish faster (you will see the difference in affect looking at 8f turf race shape). The plot below shows the sectional speeds of winners, to be clear these aren’t cumulative speeds, but the speed from one sectional to the next (taking into account the distance travelled - Trakus data - for each horse), so from the start to 2f, from 2f to 3f, etc. Winners are slowing at the finish, but as significantly as seen in dirt sprints, but still slowing.

The fastest opening half mile by a horse who went on to win was by ORB on 23/02/13 in race 11, recording a speed of 59.57 feet per second. There were horses who ran faster, CERRO ran the opening 4f in a speed of 59.67 ft per second. Both horses were competing in the same STK race, potentially benefitting from a runup of 60 feet.

The plots below show the distribution of 4.5f Finishing Speeds for winners compared to “The Rest”, and the opening 4f sectionals for winners compared to “The Rest”. The distributions are similar, especially the opening 4f where winners don’t really differentiate themselves to “The Rest” as we saw in dirt sprints. Winners, on average, run the opening 4f in 48.23, compared to “The Rest”, who run the opening 4f in 48.57, a difference of 0.34 seconds. For reference, the horses leading their race after 4f run the opening 4f in an average time of 47.89. It is the ability to carry that early speed to the finish that sees winners separate themselves from “The Rest”.

Plotting the opening 4f sectional against the closing 4f Finishing Speeds the difference becomes a little more clear. Unlike dirt sprints where Winners were seen to be running faster early, over 8.5 furlong races, horses over 8.5 furlongs have time to make up ground on tiring rivals more easily that they may have lost early .

The winner with the highest 4.5f Finishing Speed was EVIL POWDER when winning a st op clm (race 2 on 16/01/13), after an opening 4f of 50.98 EVIL POWDER recorded a Finishing Speed of 1.016. The winner with the fastest opening 4f was AIR SQUADRON when winning a all op clm (race 2 on 17/04/15), recording a time of 45.66 over the opening half mile and a 4.5f Finishing Speed of 0.936.

Turf

This is the track profile for all 8.5 furlong turf races at Gulfstream Park, there are 664 races in the dataset, contested by 6371 runners. The oldest race in the dataset was run on 2012-12-01 while the most recent was run on 2015-05-17.

Summary

A few quick and dirty summary plots highlighting the different race types and field sizes. CLM races are the most common over the 8.5 furlong course, with 255 races. The average field size for 8.5 furlong races is 9.59.

Draw

The 8.5 furlong course is run round two turns, the first turn starts soon after horses jump from the starting gates, and continues for roughly 2 furlongs, the second turn ends roughly 2f from the finish line. Counting winners is the typical way to assess any draw bias, but winners ignore a large number of runners, and if there was any such bias, it should be seen across all runners, so percentage of runners beaten is to be used (gates must have a minimum of 5 runs). A fair draw would expect to see each draw beat 50% of their rivals.

The draw will likely have an impact on the distance horses travel, especially as the first turn is such a short distance from the start. Horses drawn wide (high) will likely be forced wide. Unsurprisingly, horses drawn in gate 1 travel the least distance, with an average trip of 5678.35 feet, while horses draw in gate 13 travel an average distance of 5708.07, a difference of 29.72 feet (gates must have a minimum of 5 runs used). The plot below shows boxplots of distance raced by horses in each starting gate (the large-ish number of outliers for each gate are likely due to rail adjustments).

Race Shape

Race shape on turf courses isn’t quite as predictable as on dirt courses, as the surface allows horses who started slowly to finish faster. The plot below shows the sectional speeds of winners, to be clear these aren’t cumulative speeds, but the speed from one sectional to the next (taking into account the distance travelled - Trakus data - for each horse), so from the start to 2f, from 2f to 3f, etc. Winners are still typically running slower over the final 0.5f but the fall off isn’t as sharp as seen on dirt, and the middle part of the race from 2f to ~7.5f sees runners showing consistent speed.

The fastest opening half mile by a horse who went on to win was by KEY TO POWER on 26/02/14 in race 10, recording a speed of 59.66 feet per second. There were horses who ran faster, HARD ENOUGH ran the opening 4f in a speed of 61.37 ft per second.
